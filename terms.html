Description of Service

SilvaCruise is a mobile application for timber cruising, forest mensuration, and forestry field data collection. It provides tools for basal area estimation, volume calculations, statistical analysis, and reference materials for professional foresters.

Professional Use Disclaimer

While the calculations and reference data are based on established forestry methods and published sources (including USFS, WA DNR, and Cal Fire references), all outputs are estimates and approximations — not exact measurements. The app does not replace professional judgment. All timber cruise results, volume estimates, defect assessments, and statistical analyses should be reviewed by a qualified forester before use in official reports, timber sale appraisals, contract administration, or regulatory submissions.

Accuracy of Calculations & Estimates

SilvaCruise uses standard forestry formulas, published volume tables, and established mensuration methods. However, all outputs are estimates, not exact measurements. Users should understand the following limitations:

Volume estimates are derived from regionally averaged volume tables and form class assumptions (e.g., Scribner Decimal C, Form Class 78). Actual merchantable volume depends on individual tree form, taper, bucking decisions, mill specifications, and scaling practices. Cruise volume estimates routinely differ from scaled volume at the mill.

Defect and cull estimates are visual approximations guided by general indicators. Actual internal defect can only be determined by cutting the tree. Cull percentages entered by the user are professional judgments, not precise measurements, and deduction standards vary by agency and region.

Growth and yield projections are based on historical yield tables and average site conditions. Actual growth varies by site quality, genetics, climate, disturbance history, and management regime. Projections should be treated as planning-level estimates, not guarantees of future stand conditions.

Statistical estimates (sampling error, confidence intervals, sample size calculations) are based on standard statistical theory and are only as reliable as the underlying sampling design, field measurements, and assumptions of the sampling method.

Reference data including species characteristics, bark factors, slope correction factors, and conversion values are derived from published sources and represent general averages. Local conditions may differ.

No software can guarantee perfect accuracy in all field conditions.
